Change in Inflection, Tonality, and Volume.

Somewhere along the line, we started applying human psychology to our dogs. It seemed “nicer” to seduce them with treats rather than snap a leash when they acted up. We started speaking to them as we speak to our babies, in a sweet, loving tone. We stopped using our bodies and energy to signal our dogs, assuming they’d understand what to do by our verbal cues alone. Even when they did something to displease us, we trained ourselves to remain loving and patient and avoid expressing any energy of frustration.
